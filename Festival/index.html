Festival

Lohri

The festival of Lohri is celebrated in the western state of Punjab and Haryana. Festival of Lohri is celebrated a day before Makar Sankranti. It is celebrated as a harvest festival of Punjab. It is celebrated by eating grains and roasted corn produced by the harvest. A dish prepared from mustard green (Sarson ka saag) and Makki roti (cornbread) is prepared and savored. Around fifteen days before the festival young boys and girls roam around the villages, collecting logs and wood for the bonfire. Grains and jaggery are also collected and sold. The money thus collected is distributed among the group. A bonfire is made after sunset and people gather around it, sing and dance. It is a kind of winter solstice celebration.

Muharram

Muharram is one of the important months for Muslims. It is the first month of the Islamic calendar. Muslims believe the month is a blessed month like Ramzan. The month is associated with the many stories in Islam. The 10th day of Muharram is known as the day of Ashura. Most Muslims fast on this day. Muslims consider the month of Muharram a month to mourn Imam Hussainâ€™s martyrdom in the Battle of Karbala. Muslims believe that fasting on the 10th of Muharram is the best fast after Ramadan. Muharram marks the beginning of the Islamic New Year. Generally, the tenth day in the month of Muharram is the day that is very significant for Muslims. It is celebrated by both Sunni and Shia Muslims all over the world.

Diwali

Diwali is one of the most favorite festival of everyone. It is a most enjoyable, sacred and loveliest festival in the Hindu religion. It is celebrated every year all through the country as well as abroad (by the Indian people) with great joy and enthusiasm. Everyone (especially kids) waits for this festival with much keen. This festival is celebrated from the ancient time to mark the happy return of lord Rama with his wife and brother to his Kingdom (Ayodhya) after long fourteen years of exile.

Christmas

Christmas is celebrated on December 25 every year, and it is a cultural festival of the Christian community. It marks the birth of Jesus to Mother Mary. It is said that Jesus was born in a stable and was a good shepherd. The life of Jesus was one of hardships and sacrifices, and Christmas aims to acknowledge that fact. Christmas is a public holiday in all the countries and is celebrated by non-Christians too as it is a festival that integrates people of all cultures.

Eid

Eid is the most important festival celebrated by Muslims around the world. One is Eid-ul-Fitr or Eid and the other is Eid-ul-Azha or Bakr-Eid. Eid-ul-Fitr or Eid is a festival that marks the end of the month of Ramadan. Ramadan is the holy month of fasting for Muslim. It is the ninth month in the Muslim Calendar. After the completion of the month, when the new moon is seen, the celebration of Eid gets to start.

Dushresra

Dussehra also is known as Vijayadashami is one of the biggest festivals of Hindus in India. Some other countries like Sri Lanka, Mauritius, and Malaysia also celebrate it but have their own reasons behind it. As per the Hindu calendar, we celebrate it on the 10th day of Ashwin month in the Shukla paksha. While talking about an English Calendar, every year we celebrate this occasion between September and October.
